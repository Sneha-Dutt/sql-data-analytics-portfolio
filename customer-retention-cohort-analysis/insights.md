# Insights

1. Orders increase significantly during certain months, indicating seasonal demand.

2. Revenue concentration is highly skewed, with a small number of customers contributing a significant portion of total revenue.

3. The majority of customers place only a single order, indicating low repeat purchase behavior.

4. Customers with higher lifetime value typically make multiple purchases, highlighting the importance of retention strategies.

5. Average order value remains relatively stable across customers, suggesting that revenue growth depends more on increasing purchase frequency.

6. Segmenting customers based on lifetime value could enable targeted marketing strategies to improve retention and revenue.
